What I Do

Causal Inference from Observational Data

When randomized experiments aren't feasible, I use econometric techniques—difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, synthetic controls—to estimate causal effects from the data you already have. I've applied these methods to questions in public policy, labor economics, and product analytics.

Example clients: Veo (micromobility), blockchain startup, Alameda County DA's Office

Survey Design, Analysis, and Visualization

End-to-end survey research: instrument design, fielding strategy, statistical analysis, and presentation of findings. I've managed studies with 5,000+ respondents and delivered results to both internal stakeholders and public audiences.

Example clients: Government Executive Media Group, Veo, California Student Aid Commission

Policy Research and Data Journalism

Turning complex data into clear, public-facing analysis. I've produced health workforce reports used by state agencies, investigative data journalism that won industry awards, and policy briefs for think tanks.

Example clients: D.C. Policy Center, Waste Dive, New America, Healthforce Center at UCSF

Labor Market and Workforce Analytics

Research on AI's impact on labor demand, skills forecasting, and workforce trends. Currently working with a leading labor market research institute on these questions.

Example clients: The Burning Glass Institute (ongoing)
