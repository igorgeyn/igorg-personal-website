Current Research

"Electoral Reform Without Representation: Voting-Rights Enforcement and Racial Discipline Gaps in California Schools" Working paper

Using CVRA-induced transitions to single-member districts across 721 California school districts, I show that electoral reform did not narrow the Black–White suspension gap—an informative null explained by an asymmetric first stage: reform raised Hispanic, not Black, board representation. Minority representation is not fungible across groups.
