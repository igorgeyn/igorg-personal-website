3. VOLTAGE-HFTD RELATIONSHIP ANALYSIS
------------------------------------------------------------
HFTD % by increasing voltage (statewide):
  Sub-trans (<115 kV)        24.3%
  115-161 kV                 20.0%
  230 kV class               27.7%
  345 kV                      8.9%
  500 kV                     21.9%

Relationship: NON-MONOTONIC (no clear voltage-HFTD pattern)

The hypothesis that higher-voltage lines have more HFTD exposure
is NOT supported. 230 kV class has the highest exposure (28%),
while 345 kV has the lowest (9%). This likely reflects routing:
  - 345 kV lines run through the Central Valley (low fire risk)
  - 230 kV lines serve mountain communities (higher fire risk)

5. KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR WRITE-UP
------------------------------------------------------------
• No clear voltage-HFTD relationship exists in the data
• 230 kV class has highest statewide exposure (28%)
• 345 kV has lowest exposure (9%) — Central Valley routing
• PG&E's network is more fire-exposed than statewide average
  (30% vs 23% overall)
• PG&E 500 kV average: 24.7% in HFTD
• Manning-Metcalf at 50-60% is 2.2x the PG&E 500 kV average
• This makes Manning-Metcalf an outlier even among 500 kV lines
