# Publication Figures: Data Center Fire Risk Study

Five figures for the policy research paper on fire risk exposure of California's data center transmission buildout. All generated by scripts in `scripts/visualizations/`.

## Figures

### Figure 1: Corridor Route Map (Hero Chart)
**File:** `fig1_corridor_route_map.png`
**Script:** `scripts/visualizations/fig1_corridor_map.py`
**Data:** HIFLD transmission lines (`raw/hifld/transmission_lines_corridor.geojson`), CPUC HFTD v3 boundaries (`raw/hftd/*.gdb`), route metadata (`processed/hifld_corridor_routes.csv`)
**Description:** Map of the Manning-Metcalf 500 kV corridor showing HFTD Tier 2/3 fire threat zones, the existing 500 kV route proxy (60% HFTD), straight-line approximation (85% HFTD), 230 kV alternates, five substations, and city labels. Contextily basemap for geographic orientation.

### Figure 2: Segment-by-Segment Fire Risk Profile
**File:** `fig2_segment_fire_profile.png`
**Script:** `scripts/visualizations/fig2_segment_profile.py`
**Data:** `processed/hifld_corridor_segments.csv` (1 km segments for the Moss Landing-Metcalf 500 kV route)
**Description:** Strip chart showing HFTD tier classification for each kilometer along the route, from south (Manning/Moss Landing) to north (Metcalf). Annotated with geographic features: Pajaro Gap, Diablo Range foothills, Coyote Valley, Metcalf approach. Shows 60% of corridor in fire threat districts.

### Figure 3: Substation Fire Exposure Gradient
**File:** `fig3_substation_fire_gradient.png`
**Script:** `scripts/visualizations/fig3_substation_gradient.py`
**Data:** `processed/hftd_overlay_results.csv` (5 km and 10 km buffer analysis for five substations)
**Description:** Horizontal bar chart comparing HFTD exposure across five substations ordered south to north (Manning to Newark). Paired bars for 5 km and 10 km buffers, stacked by Tier 2/3. Demonstrates the fire risk gradient from high (Manning, 78%) to minimal (Newark, <1%).

### Figure 4: PSPS Event Timeline
**File:** `fig4_psps_corridor_timeline.png`
**Script:** `scripts/visualizations/fig4_psps_timeline.py`
**Data:** `G:/My Drive/Personal/energy_analysis/raw_data/psps_cleaned.csv` (cleaned CPUC PSPS event data)
**Description:** Timeline of PSPS de-energization events on corridor circuits (2019-2025). Transmission circuits at top, distribution below. Bar width proportional to event duration. Right-side axis shows total customer-hours per circuit. Highlights Morgan Hill-2111 as chronic (5 events).

### Figure 5: Data Center Load Growth
**File:** `fig5_dc_load_growth.png`
**Script:** `scripts/visualizations/fig5_load_growth.py`
**Data:** `processed/cec_datacenter_load_annual_caiso.csv`, `processed/cec_load_components_annual_peaks_caiso.csv`
**Description:** CEC 2025 IEPR forecast of data center peak load (2025-2040) with min-max range band. Secondary axis shows DC as % of CAISO baseline consumption. Annotated milestones: 1,774 MW by 2030, 4,910 MW plateau. Transmission cost callout ($500-700M).

## Rebuilding

To regenerate all figures:
```
python scripts/visualizations/build_all.py
```

## Style

All figures use a shared configuration (`scripts/visualizations/style_config.py`) with:
- Fire-themed color palette (deep red for Tier 3, burnt orange for Tier 2, cool grays for outside HFTD)
- Consistent font sizing (14pt titles, 11pt labels, 10pt ticks, 9pt annotations)
- 200 DPI output
- Minimal chartjunk styling
