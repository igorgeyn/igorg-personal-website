5. CONTEXT: MANNING-METCALF 500 kV CORRIDOR
------------------------------------------------------------
  Corridor length:        ~55 km
  HFTD exposure:          50-60% (from route modeling analysis)
  CA 500 kV average:      21.9% HFTD
  Comparison:             Manning-Metcalf is 2.5x more exposed than average 500 kV

  The Manning-Metcalf corridor is not an outlier in having HFTD exposure —
  but it IS unusually high exposure for a NEW 500 kV line serving data centers.

6. IMPLICATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
  • Generation developers successfully AVOIDED fire zones (6% vs 15% of CA)
  • But they cannot avoid fire zones for TRANSMISSION (23% exposure)
  • PG&E transmission has 30% HFTD exposure (5,000+ km in fire zones)
  • SDG&E has 65% exposure — nearly all of their network crosses HFTD
  • The data center buildout will add load, requiring new transmission
    that must traverse these same fire-prone corridors
